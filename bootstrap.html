    Before humans began to walk upright, and spark new fires for the future; Beanstalks stretched to the heavens. Religions were built around this Gaia-colored staircase. What does scaling new heights do for our evolution?

    In fables across the globe - one of the earliest is centered around a single mother with her only son, Jack, and their prized cow, Maude. Jack sold Maude to market for a few magic beans. Mother was not very happy with Jack at all She sent him to bed without supper, and tossed the beans out back with no second thought. Overnight it sprouted beyond the clouds. eventually leading to adventure and gold fortunes. Beanstalks have also been popular with young adults, and middle-aged fans. Pictured in Vanity Fair, and Wired, Beanstalks have been growing in the public consciousness, and don't seem to be slowing anytime soon.

    All parts of the Beanstalk have proven useful to make everything from clothes to paper products. Even the mineral matter at the stalk roots are used for building new batteries for emerging technologies.

    Beanstalk Nutrition

    Gone are the days of scarce resources. The Beanstalk is edible, and most parts can be eaten raw. The root systems have to be heated to break through the protective layer that would be tough to digest. Beyond that - have at it! The leaves yeild more protien than broccoli, and Veal combined.
